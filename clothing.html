Distorted Reality

Clothing that bends perception and warps space around the wearer.

The Concept

Distorted Reality examines our relationship with visual perception and spatial awareness. Each piece in this collection is designed to create optical illusions that confuse the eye and challenge conventional understanding of form and silhouette.

Inspired by the works of M.C. Escher and the philosophical concept of phenomenology, these garments explore how clothing can alter not just how we are perceived, but how we perceive ourselves and the space around us.

The collection features garments with impossible geometries, patterns that shift depending on viewing angle, and structural elements that seem to defy gravity. Fabrics have been developed with specialized printing techniques that create depth where there is none, and flatness where volume should exist.

Designer's Notes

"I've always been fascinated by how the mind processes visual information and fills in gaps to create meaning. With Distorted Reality, I wanted to exploit this tendency—to create clothing that forces the viewer to constantly reinterpret what they're seeing."

"The production process was extraordinarily challenging. We had to develop entirely new pattern-cutting techniques and work with specialist printers to achieve the visual effects we were after. Many pieces went through dozens of iterations before the illusion worked correctly from all angles."

"My favorite piece is probably the 'Recursive Jacket,' which appears to fold in on itself infinitely when viewed in the right light. We used a combination of strategically placed mirrors and mathematically precise geometric patterns to create the effect."

Innovative Materials

Perceptual Silk

A custom-developed fabric printed with patterns that create the illusion of movement when static. The printing process involves seven layers of specialized inks that respond differently to various lighting conditions.

Dimensional Wool

A wool blend treated with heat-reactive elements that shift the apparent depth of the material, creating areas that seem to recede or project from the garment's surface.

Gravitational Cotton

A heavy cotton treated with nano-scale structures that reflect light in unexpected ways, making the fabric appear to fold in impossible directions.

Mirror Mesh

A lightweight mesh embedded with microscopic reflective elements that create the illusion of transparency or opacity depending on viewing angle.
